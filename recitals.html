Why Five

More Chances to Feel What It's Like.

Many students need more than one big performance moment a year. Regular recital opportunities help the stage become familiar instead of intimidating.

The first recital can feel big. The next one feels a little more familiar. Over time, students begin to understand that performing is not only about nerves — it is also about sharing what they have worked for. That shift carries into every lesson.

Recitals are free for families to attend. Students are never required to perform, but most want to — especially once they've done it once.

Students at CSM Recitals

Every Instrument. Every Level.

From a student playing their first simple piece to an advanced player performing a full movement — recitals at CSM are for everyone.

Preparation
Teachers prepare students well before the date
Students know what they're playing, how to walk on and off stage, and what to expect. Nobody is pushed to perform before they're ready.
Atmosphere
Supportive, not competitive
Recitals at CSM are not competitions. Students are there to share what they've been working on. Families cheer for everyone — including students they don't know.
Format
Live performance with family and friends
Held at real performance venues across Greater Cincinnati. Students perform in front of a live audience — not just a video camera or an empty room.
Cost
Free for all CSM families
There is no admission fee, no recital fee, and no costume requirement. Families are welcome to come, bring guests, and stay for the whole program.
Frequency
Throughout the year, across CSM locations
Recitals are held throughout the year so students have regular chances to perform without waiting for one big annual event.
Who Can Perform
Any CSM student who is enrolled and ready
All instruments, all levels. Students typically begin performing in their first year — often after just a few months of lessons.

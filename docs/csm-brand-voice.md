# CSM Brand Voice

## Purpose

This document defines how Cincinnati School of Music should sound across the website, Resource Hub, landing pages, teacher recruiting pages, parent-facing content, and future platform features.

The goal is not to sound like a marketing company.

The goal is to sound like Cincinnati School of Music.

---

# Core Identity

We are a large, established music school.

We are also warm and human.

We are not a startup.

We are not a conservatory.

We are not a franchise.

We are not a corporate chain.

We are a real music school serving real families.

Founded in 2012.

Five locations.

60+ teachers.

1,000+ active students.

Those facts should quietly inform the writing.

They should not be shouted.

---

# Voice Characteristics

## Warm

Friendly.

Human.

Welcoming.

Never cold or institutional.

## Direct

Answer the question.

Do not dance around the point.

Do not bury the answer under marketing language.

## Specific

Use real examples.

Use real situations.

Use real parent concerns.

Avoid vague statements.

## Experienced

We should sound like people who have helped thousands of students.

Not like people trying to prove they know what they are talking about.

## Calm

Confident without bragging.

Professional without sounding corporate.

---

# What We Sound Like

"We teach a lot of students."

"We see this question often."

"Many beginners do well on a quality digital piano."

"Most families worry about this at first."

"There isn't one right answer."

"It depends on the student."

"Consistency matters more than talent."

---

# What We Do NOT Sound Like

## Generic AI

Avoid:

* unlock your potential
* nurturing environment
* embark on a musical journey
* personalized instruction
* world-class
* transformative experience
* whether you're just starting out or...
* passionate instructors
* discover your passion
* unleash creativity

## Corporate

Avoid:

* solutions
* stakeholders
* leverage
* optimize
* synergize
* ecosystem

## Hype

Avoid:

* best
* premier
* elite
* unmatched
* revolutionary

Unless there is an objective reason to use the term.

# Parent Resource Hub Rules

The Resource Hub exists to help parents.

It is not disguised advertising.

Every article should pass this test:

Would this article still be useful if the reader never enrolled at CSM?

If no, rewrite it.

Helpful first.

CSM second.

CTA third.

---

# How We Mention CSM

Good:

"At CSM, Intro Lessons are designed to help families determine whether lessons are a good fit."

Good:

"Many of our students begin on digital pianos."

Good:

"We've seen students succeed with a wide range of practice habits and schedules."

Bad:

"CSM's award-winning system unlocks each student's full potential."

Bad:

"CSM is the best choice for every family."

---

# Competitors

Never attack competitors.

Never imply other schools are bad.

It is acceptable to acknowledge that different schools serve different families.

Example:

"Some schools focus heavily on competitive performance. Others focus on group instruction. Some families prefer working with an independent teacher. The right fit depends on what you're looking for."

---

# Teacher Recruiting Voice

Teacher-site content should be honest.

Do not overpromise.

Do not imply every teacher will immediately build a full schedule.

Do not use recruiting hype.

State how the school actually operates.

The question being answered is:

Why would a good teacher enjoy working here over the next teaching job?

Answer with facts.

* W-2 employment
* organized systems
* facilities
* enrollment support
* marketing support
* professional culture
* long-term stability

---

# CSM Writing Test

Before publishing, ask:

1. Does this sound like a real person?
2. Would a parent find this useful?
3. Is the answer specific?
4. Are we saying what we actually believe?
5. Would we still be comfortable saying this five years from now?

If any answer is no, revise.

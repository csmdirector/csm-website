Book Intro
Piano at CSM

Private Lessons. Thoughtfully Taught.

Piano is the foundation of musical understanding — and one of the most rewarding instruments a student can learn at any age. Our teachers work with beginners as young as five and adults picking up the instrument for the first time.

With piano teachers across all five locations, families have more ways to find the right teacher and a lesson time that fits real life. Every lesson is private, and teachers shape the work around the student sitting in front of them. Instructors are chosen for their ability to keep students engaged and moving forward — not just technically proficient.

All Skill Levels

Where Are You Starting From?

01
Beginner
No experience needed. Note reading, hand position, basic technique. Method books chosen to keep young students engaged. The goal from day one is making music, not just practicing exercises.
02
Intermediate
Students with some foundation work on expanding repertoire, scales, music theory, and expression. Lessons move beyond method books into pieces the student actually wants to learn.
03
Advanced
Technique refinement, complex repertoire, musical artistry. College auditions, competition preparation, and serious individual study are all part of what our faculty supports at this level.
04
Adult Beginners
It is never too late to start. Jazz standards, classical, pop, show tunes. Lessons move at your pace, on your terms.
05
Young Students (Ages 5–7)
Short, engaging lessons built around fun repertoire and foundational skills. Patience, consistency, and the right teacher matter most at this age.
06
Styles We Teach
Classical, contemporary, jazz, rock, pop, Christian, blues. Students are encouraged to bring the music they love. A teacher who teaches what you want to play keeps students engaged.

How Lessons Work

Simple Structure. Clear Expectations.

All piano lessons are private, one-on-one sessions. You choose the lesson length that fits your student's age, attention span, and goals. Younger beginners typically start at 30 minutes. Intermediate and advanced students benefit from longer sessions.

Lessons run seven days a week across our network. Multiple family members can take lessons at the same location on the same day — a practical detail that matters for busy families.

Cincinnati School of Music

60+ Teachers. One at a Time.

Every lesson at CSM is private and one-on-one. With 60+ teachers, we can look for the right fit for your child — not simply the first open time. Our office handles the match so you don't have to sort through a roster on your own.

How It Works

We Help You Find the Right Fit.

Families are not expected to sort through dozens of profiles and guess who might be a good match for their child. That is a lot to ask of a parent, and it is not how we want families to start.

When you enroll, our office matches your child with a teacher based on instrument, age, experience level, goals, personality, location, and schedule. If the fit isn't right, we work with you to find someone who is.

The right teacher can be the reason a student keeps showing up, keeps practicing, and starts to see music as part of who they are.

What goes into the match
Instrument and level
Whether your child is picking up an instrument for the first time or has been playing for years, we match them with a teacher who knows that starting point well.
Age and personality
A five-year-old and a fifteen-year-old need different things from a teacher. So does a kid who needs patience and a kid who needs to be pushed.
Goals and timeline
A student preparing for school band has different needs than one learning songs for fun. We account for that when making the match.
Location and schedule
Five locations, seven days a week. We work to find a teacher, time, and studio that fits naturally into the family's week.
Teaching style
Some students need structure. Some need encouragement. Some need a teacher who leads with humor. We pay attention to that.
Long-term fit
The best teacher relationships last years. We are looking for that kind of fit — not just someone to fill the time slot.
What We Hire For

More Than Musicianship.

Strong musicianship matters, but teaching takes more than being able to play. Our teachers are selected for both — and the teaching ability is what we look hardest at.

We look for instructors who pay attention to the student in front of them. Who adjust when something isn't working. Who show up week after week genuinely invested in a student's progress and not just in getting through the lesson.

That attention is what turns weekly lessons into a habit families keep for years.

One on One

Every Lesson Is Private.

There are no group classes in private lessons. Every student gets their teacher's full attention for the full lesson — no sharing time, no waiting, no being compared to anyone else.

That kind of attention gives students room to grow at their own pace. Students know what they're working on. Teachers know where each student is week to week. Parents can see the difference.

Our Faculty

Strong Teachers. Every Instrument.

Professional performers University-trained musicians Experienced private teachers Classroom educators Specialists in beginners Advanced student instructors

With 60+ teachers across five locations, CSM has the depth to staff every instrument well — and to match families with someone who fits, not just someone who is available. Teachers are hired for their ability to teach and connect, not credentials alone.

When the Fit Is Right

Students Who Keep Going.

The families who stay at CSM longest almost always say the same thing: their child loves their teacher. Not just the instrument — the teacher specifically.

That relationship is what we are building toward when we make the match. Music lessons work best when a student looks forward to the lesson, feels comfortable being wrong, and trusts that their teacher is in their corner.

We take that seriously from the first lesson.
